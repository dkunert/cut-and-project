Subject: Question on rational cut and project gap periods

Dear Dr Koivusalo,

I am an independent researcher and have completed a manuscript on explicit
minimal-period formulas for one-dimensional rational cut and project strip
projections. Your 2016 paper "Gaps problems and frequencies of patches in cut and
project sets" (with Haynes, Sadun, and Walton) is the closest published work I know
on gaps in this setting, and your more recent cut-and-project programme — the
polytopal-window papers on complexity and linear repetitivity, and the recent paper
on substitutionality — is the body of work I found myself comparing against most
closely. I would be very grateful for your view on whether the result below is
already in the literature.

The setting is a strip projection with rational slope α/β (gcd(α, β) = 1) and window
parameter ω ≥ 0. With

    N = ⌊ωα⌋ + ⌊ωβ⌋ + 1,    D = α² + β²,

after orthogonally projecting the accepted lattice points and sorting them along the
line, the gap sequence has minimal period

  • multiset convention (projected values counted with multiplicity):
        λ_multiset = N      if D ∤ N,
        λ_multiset = N / D  if D | N;

  • set convention (multiplicities discarded):
        λ_set = N    if N < D,
        λ_set = 1    if N ≥ D.

In particular λ_set | λ_multiset, with equality if and only if N ≤ D. The proof
reduces the geometry to the distribution of N consecutive residue classes modulo D
under multiplication by the unit −αβ⁻¹. A complementary result shows that irrational
slopes give aperiodic gap sequences, closing the rational/irrational dichotomy.

Since the earlier drafts I have carried out a thorough, AI-assisted prior-art search,
documented in a companion note I am happy to share: arXiv, zbMATH Open (title, review,
and MSC level), dblp/DGCI–DGMM, and the digital-geometry and aperiodic-order monograph
literature, together with a direct reading of your group's recent cut-and-project
papers. I did not find this exact result stated anywhere. The nearest match in
vocabulary is the codimension-one linear-repetitivity criterion in your "polytopal
window II" paper, but there the slope is irrational and the conclusion is a
repetitivity rate ‖αn + β‖ ≥ c/n rather than a finite period, the relevant integer is
a lattice-membership order rather than the window count N, and α² + β² does not appear
as a period modulus. (Alan Haynes kindly looked at an earlier draft and felt the
formula might be known or follow from standard number theory while the Lean
verification could be new, but did not point me to a specific source.)

To make the result readily checkable, the proof is fully formalized in Lean 4 /
Mathlib — approximately 3,760 lines across Basic.lean and Irrational.lean, with no
`sorry`, `axiom`, or `admit`. The manuscript is attached as a PDF, and the Lean source
(tagged v1.4-arxiv, matching the line numbers in Table 1 of the paper) is at
https://github.com/dkunert/cut-and-project/tree/v1.4-arxiv.

I would be grateful for any brief indication of whether this formula, or an equivalent
lemma, appears somewhere I have missed.
