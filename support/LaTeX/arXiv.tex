\section*{Purpose}

This note collects practical suggestions for submitting the manuscript

\begin{center}
\emph{Period Formulas for Rational Cut-and-Project Strip Projections:
Multiset and Set Cases}
\end{center}

to arXiv.  It lists suitable subject categories, possible expert readers,
and a suggested first-contact email.

The paper proves two complementary closed-form period formulas (one for
the multiset of projected gap values, one for the underlying set) and
shows that the set period is always at most the multiset period, with
equality precisely when \(N \le D\).  The algebraic core of both
theorems is formalised in Lean~4 (\(\sim\)2{,}850 lines, no
\texttt{sorry}, \texttt{admit}, or \texttt{axiom}).

\section*{Can an independent researcher submit to arXiv?}

Yes.  An independent researcher may submit to arXiv.  The issue is not the
lack of a university affiliation, but the endorsement requirement.  A new
submitter normally needs endorsement for the relevant arXiv category or
subject class.

The affiliation may simply be written as

\begin{verbatim}
Independent Researcher
\end{verbatim}

in the paper.

\section*{Recommended arXiv categories}

The paper is not best framed primarily as deep number theory.  It uses
number-theoretic tools such as congruences, residue classes, and coprimality,
but its main object is a gap sequence arising from a geometric and
combinatorial construction.

Recommended categories:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Primary: \texttt{math.CO} or \texttt{math.DS}.
    \item Possible cross-listing: \texttt{math.NT} or \texttt{math.MG}.
\end{itemize}

A reasonable first choice is

\begin{center}
\texttt{math.CO}
\end{center}

because the proofs are essentially combinatorial and residue-class based:
both the multiset formula and the set-versus-multiset dichotomy reduce
to counting cumulative residue classes modulo \(D = \alpha^2 + \beta^2\).
If the paper is presented more strongly as a symbolic-dynamical
gap-sequence result, then \texttt{math.DS} is also plausible.

\section*{How to approach potential endorsers or expert readers}

It is better not to send a cold message that immediately asks for endorsement.
A more professional strategy is:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Send a concise email with the manuscript attached or linked.
    \item Explain the result in two or three sentences.
    \item Ask whether the result appears suitable as an arXiv preprint and
    whether the recipient knows prior literature containing the same formula.
    \item Only if the response is positive, ask whether the person would be
    willing to endorse the arXiv submission.
\end{enumerate}

This is less intrusive and more likely to receive a serious response.

\section*{Suggested first-contact email}

\begin{verbatim}
Subject: Short note on rational cut-and-project gap periods

Dear Professor [Name],

I am an independent researcher working on a short note about rational
one-dimensional cut-and-project strip projections.

The paper proves two complementary closed-form formulas for the minimal
period of the projected gap sequence.  For coprime positive integers
alpha, beta and window parameter omega >= 0, set

    N = floor(omega alpha) + floor(omega beta) + 1,
    D = alpha^2 + beta^2.

Counting projected values with multiplicity gives

    lambda_multiset = N        if D does not divide N,
    lambda_multiset = N / D    if D divides N,

and discarding multiplicities gives

    lambda_set = N             if N < D,
    lambda_set = 1             if N >= D.

In particular lambda_set <= lambda_multiset, with equality iff N <= D.

The proofs are elementary and reduce the problem to the distribution of
consecutive residue classes modulo D.  The algebraic core of both
theorems has also been formalised in Lean 4 (no sorry, admit, or axiom).

Since your work is close to cut-and-project sets / gap problems / symbolic
dynamics, I would be grateful if you were willing to take a brief look at the
manuscript.  In particular, I would appreciate knowing whether the result
appears to be a reasonable arXiv preprint and whether you are aware of prior
literature containing this exact formula.

I fully understand if you do not have time.

Best regards,
Dirk Kunert
\end{verbatim}

\section*{Checklist before contacting anyone}

Before sending the manuscript to potential readers, check the following:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The paper compiles without errors.
    \item The title names both the multiset and the set case.
    \item The abstract states both period formulas and the inequality
    \(\lambda_{\mathrm{set}} \le \lambda_{\mathrm{multiset}}\).
    \item The setup contains an explicit multiplicity convention and is
    clear that the set case discards multiplicities.
    \item The bi-infinite nondecreasing enumeration of projected values is
    justified for both the multiset and the set side.
    \item The proof of \(\tilde p_s^{(i+N)}=\tilde p_s^{(i)}+D\) is explicit.
    \item The degenerate case \(D\mid N\) clearly explains the role of zero
    gaps.
    \item The set-side dichotomy at \(N=D\) (period \(N\) below, period
    \(1\) at and above) is stated as a separate theorem.
    \item Table~1 lists the line-by-line correspondence between paper
    results and Lean declarations and the cited line numbers are correct.
    \item The Lean formalisation is described precisely and not overstated:
    \(\sim\)2{,}850 lines in \texttt{Basic.lean}, no \texttt{sorry},
    \texttt{admit}, or \texttt{axiom}; both
    \texttt{main\_theorem\_concrete} (multiset) and
    \texttt{set\_main\_theorem\_concrete} (set) are unconditional.
    \item AI systems are not listed as co-authors.
    \item AI assistance is mentioned only in the acknowledgements, with the
    author taking full responsibility for the mathematical content.
    \item The bibliography is cleaned up, preferably replacing Wikipedia with
    a mathematical reference for the three-gap theorem.
    \item The GitHub repository (\texttt{cut-and-project}) builds cleanly:
    \texttt{lake build} succeeds against the pinned toolchain
    (\texttt{leanprover/lean4:v4.29.1}, Mathlib commit
    \texttt{5e932f97}\dots) and \texttt{verify\_paper.py} passes against
    the CSV data in \texttt{tests/}.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Practical recommendation}

The best first step is to contact one or two people from the closest group,
for example Alan Haynes and Henna Koivusalo, with a short email and the
manuscript attached.  If there is no response, proceed to James Walton,
Val\'{e}rie Berth\'{e}, or S\'{e}bastien Labb\'{e}.

The request should initially be for feedback on whether the results appear
suitable for arXiv and whether either formula (multiset or set) is known
in the literature, not immediately for endorsement.  The set-versus-multiset
dichotomy is the more distinctive contribution and is worth highlighting
explicitly when summarising the paper.
